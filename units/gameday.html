About this stay

Both suites, the outdoor bar, the back lot — opened up for the weekends the city is alive.

Gameday is the whole social side of Faye Social House: the upstairs lock-off suite (The Balcony, king), the ground-floor lock-off suite (The Front Porch, queen), the outdoor bar and grill, and the back lot built for 15 cars. It's a different listing from the suites individually because it only opens a handful of weekends a year — Razorback home games, Bikes Blues & BBQ, fall festival weekends, the kind of dates where you want the whole experience, not just a bed.

On those weekends Fayetteville hums. Students on the sidewalk, tailgaters in the alley, a freight train cutting through twice a day, fight-song faintly drifting up from the stadium half a mile south. We open the porch, the upstairs balcony, the side yard, and the bar — and your group has the run of all of it. Sleeps four indoors, hosts a bigger crowd outside.

Park up to 15 cars in the back lot — your group, your guests, whoever's joining for the cookout. Smart locks on both suites; one keypad code each. Self check-in any time after 3 PM. Both kitchenettes stocked with coffee, the grill primed, ice on request. There's a smart TV in each suite if the game starts before you can walk over.

Pricing reflects the weekend it's covering. Minimum $250/night, real numbers usually higher around marquee games. The main house is rented long-term — Gameday includes the two lock-off suites and the outdoor common space, not the main residence. Locked doors keep the boundaries clean.
